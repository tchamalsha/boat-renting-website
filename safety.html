Saltwater Skippers Zego 300 Boat Safety Guide.

Introduction

Operating a Zego 300 small watercraft is a fun and exciting way to explore the beautiful waters of Newfoundland and Labrador! This guide will help you understand how to operate the Zego 300 safely and confidently.

1. Operator Requirements

  • Renting: Must be 18 years or older.
  • Guided Tours: Must be 16 years or older. Parental consent required for independent operation.
  • Orientation: Our team provides a quick and easy training before departure.

2. Personal Safety Equipment

  • Wear a Transport Canada-approved life jacket (PFD).
  • Use the provided safety whistle for emergencies.
  • Protect yourself with sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat.

3. Pre-Departure Checklist

  • Inspect the boat for visible damage.
  • Ensure the boat is fueled.
  • Check life jackets, safety whistle, and paddle.
  • Review weather conditions.

4. Operating the Zego 300 Safely

  • Start the engine in neutral and attach the safety lanyard.
  • Steer like a bicycle and ease into the throttle.
  • Maintain a safe distance from other boats and shorelines.
  • Dock slowly with small steering adjustments.

5. Emergency Procedures

  • If the engine stops, use a paddle or signal for help.
  • In case of a capsize, stay with the boat and signal for help.
  • For man overboard, stop the engine and assist immediately.

6. Environmental Responsibility

  • Respect marine life and avoid littering.
  • Report any fuel or oil leaks immediately.
  • Follow the principle: leave only ripples!

7. Rules and Regulations

  • Follow all local boating laws and Saltwater Skippers guidelines.
  • No alcohol or drugs while operating the boat.
  • Maintain safe speeds and avoid reckless driving.

8. Additional Safety Tips

  • Inform someone of your route and return time.
  • Monitor weather conditions and return if it worsens.
  • Stay hydrated and enjoy your ride responsibly.
